What is Kanban ?

The Concept of Kanban was originated in 1940s by Toyota. In Japanese "Kanban" means "Visual Signal" or Visual Card. The Main Purpose of Kanban is to help and improve manufacturing efficiency and create a transparent work process for everybody. Kanban visualizes both the process (the workflow) and the actual work passing through that process. The goal of Kanban is to identify potential bottlenecks in your process and fix them so work can flow through it cost-effectively at an optimal speed or throughput.

6 Core Practices of Kanban

  1. Visualize the Workflow
  2. Limit Work in Progress
  3. Manging Workflows
  4. Implement explicit policies
  5. Allow Room for Feedback
  6. Always search for improvement
